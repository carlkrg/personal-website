How to Protect from Tracking and Advertisements on the Internet?

First, check your current protection level by using the tool Cover Your Tracks by the Electronic Frontier Foundation. If the results indicate gaps in protection against web tracking, here are steps to improve your privacy and web experience.

1. Choose the Right Browser:

The choice of browser is crucial for privacy. Firefox, developed by the non-profit organization Mozilla, is fully open-source and is known for its focus on user privacy and security. It's widely used and trusted by the privacy-conscious community, including the Tor Project. On the other hand, browsers like Chrome are developed by companies with business models that may include monetizing user data. Safari, while offering robust privacy features, may not support all the proposed extensions.

If you choose Firefox, consider these security settings: Firefox Security Settings.

2. Install Privacy Badger:

Privacy Badger automatically blocks invisible trackers and learns to block new ones based on their behavior. It also sends signals to opt you out of data sharing and selling:

  • Privacy Badger for Firefox
  • Privacy Badger for Chrome

3. Install uBlock Origin:

uBlock Origin is a wide-spectrum content blocker focusing on efficiency:

  • uBlock Origin for Firefox
  • uBlock Origin for Chrome

4. Install Decentraleyes:

Decentraleyes protects against tracking through "free", centralized content delivery, serving local files to keep sites from breaking:

  • Decentraleyes for Firefox
  • Decentraleyes for Chrome

5. Check Again:

Rerun the test at Cover Your Tracks to see the improvements. Ideally, you should have stronger protection against web tracking.

Enjoy a more secure and private browsing experience!
